Understanding Healthy Relationships & Boundaries
A healthy relationship means both people feel free to make choices without pressure.
Your feelings and comfort matter just as much as anyone else’s.
It’s always okay to change your mind, at any time, for any reason.
If you ever feel like you “have to” do something, take a step back and ask yourself why.
🗣️ (Discuss) “There’s a difference between choosing something because you truly want to and feeling like you have to.”
Recognizing Pressure & Coercion
Watch out for guilt-tripping: “If you cared about me, you would.”
Wearing you down: Asking over and over until you say yes.
Making you feel bad for saying no: Sulking, anger, or acting distant.
Comparing you to others: “Everyone else is doing it.”
Making you feel like you’ll lose them: “If you won’t, maybe we’re not right for each other.”
🗣️ (Discuss) “If you had said no, do you think they would have been fine with it?”
🗣️ (Discuss) “Would you feel comfortable stopping next time if you changed your mind?”
🚩 If someone reacts badly to a ‘no,’ that’s a red flag.
Strengthening Your Autonomy & Boundaries
No one is entitled to your body or time.
Your feelings matter as much as theirs.
You don’t owe anyone a ‘yes,’ even if they’ve done something for you.
If someone truly cares about you, they’ll always respect a ‘no.’
🗣️ (Discuss) “Let’s say next time you’re not feeling it. How would you say ‘no’ naturally?”
🗣️ (Discuss) “If they reacted badly, what would you do?”
Handling Situations Where You Feel Unsure
If you’re not 100% comfortable, pause and reflect.
Would I still do this if I knew they were fine with me saying no?
It’s okay to step away and think before making a decision.
Trusting Your Feelings & Seeking Support
Trust your instincts—if something feels off, there’s a reason.
Healthy relationships feel safe, fun, and equal—not pressured or uncertain.
🗣️ (Discuss) “No matter what happens, you can always call someone you trust—no judgment.”
🗣️ (Discuss) “Even if you feel stuck, there is always a way out.”
Summary
Your choices are yours alone, and you deserve to be with people who respect them.
